PROJECTS

ASL-1

P1- The Anga Launch System 1 (ALS-1)-will be a 20-meter rocket powered by a single open-cycle rocket engine called Thermon, using liquid oxygen and PR1 ethanol as propellants. The open-cycle design, based on a gas-generator cycle, ensures reliable and efficient thrust during the initial ascent of the rocket. The structural components shall be realized by the use of aluminum alloys because of their good cost and widespread availability in Kenya, for ideal weight and strength balance. On the aspect of weight, the strength of Carbon Fiber is the strongest, whereas on the subject of budget or material availability on the local markets, aluminum is more suitable.

P2 -ALS-1 is going to be heavily reliant on AI for its development and operational success. Machine learning algorithms will assist the design and simulation phases, processing big data to optimize performance. It will also monitor and control the systems of the rocket in flight, making minute adjustments in real time to keep functionality at optimal levels. This integration of AI will further enhance the reliability, safety, and efficiency of the rocket, therefore allowing ALS-1 to accomplish its missions with minimal risk.

P3-The robotic arms will be used for the machining of structural components of ALS-1, improving precision and increasing production rates. The use of these robotic arms in shaping and assembling parts of the rocket ensures perfection in every piece manufactured of the rocket. Human error is at its minimum in this way, consistency is improved, and production is accelerated. Complex designs with small tolerances, possible with robotic arms, would guarantee structural integrity both at the time of launch and during flying.

P4- This will be coupled with an AFTS that can track every deviation from flight paths autonomously, which would therefore neutralize the space vehicle if this flight has a possibility of resulting in harm or damage to human life and personal properties. The AFTS forms the backbone of its safety infrastructure as it offers the autonomous speed-of-life responses to possible flight anomalies.

P5-ALS-1 will take two years in development and will make its maiden flight by the end of 2027, provided the funds are found. This will involve extensive testing and validation of all systems to make certain that the rocket is fully operational and reliable for missions that may range from satellite deployments to scientific research. Equipped with the latest technologies and designed with great caution, ALS-1 will be able to hold its own in the space industry.
